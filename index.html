About

Nikolay Nikolov

I am a student at Imperial College London pursuing MEng in Electronic and Information Engineering (with integrated BEng). Currently, I am doing the 4th year of my studies, including my Master's Thesis, as exchange student at ETH Zurich.

My main research goal is to enable robots to learn new skills on their own in order to execute complex tasks in a variety of environments. In particular, I am currently working in Deep Reinforcement Learning for Robotics. I am also interested in Deep Learning and Computer Vision.

Currently, I am part of Prof. Andreas Krause's research group at the Learning & Adaptive Systems Lab at ETH. Previously, I was part of Prof. Stefan Leutenegger's group at the Dyson Robotics Lab at Imperial. I have also worked together with Prof. Petar Kormushev from the Robot Intelligence Lab and Prof. Mirko Kovac at the Aerial Robotics Lab.

Efficient Octree-Based Volumetric SLAM Supporting Signed-Distance and Occupancy Mapping.
Emanuele Vespa, Nikolay Nikolov, Marius Grimm, Luigi Nardi, Paul H J Kelly, Stefan Leutenegger.
Under review for the IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA), 2018

We propose a bayesian formulation for fusing depth camera measurements in a volumetric occupancy octree grid. Our approach is particulary targeting SLAM applications and allows direct surface extraction from the octree. We also provide a CPU-optimized octree library for 3D reconstruction and SLAM that aims to allow implementing general-purpose real-time mapping algorithms.
