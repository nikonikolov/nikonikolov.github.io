About

Nikolay Nikolov

I am currently working as a Reinforcement Learning Researcher at Wayve.

I completed my B.Eng. and M.Eng. in Electronic and Information Engineering at Imperial College London. I spent the last year of my studies as exchange student at ETH Zurich, where I was part of Prof. Andreas Krause's research group at the Learning & Adaptive Systems Lab. Prior to that I was part of Prof. Stefan Leutenegger's group at the Dyson Robotics Lab at Imperial.

I am interested in enabling robots to learn new skills on their own in order to execute complex tasks in various real-world environments. In particular, I am interested in deep reinforcement learning, representation learning and meta learning for robotics.

Publications

2019

Urban Driving with Conditional Imitation Learning.
J Hawke*, R Shen*, C Gurau*, S Sharma*, D Reda*, N Nikolov*, P Mazur*, S Micklethwaite*, N Griffiths*, A Shah*, A Kendall*.
 Machine Learning for Autonomous Driving Workshop, NeurIPS 2019.
Under review for IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters and ICRA, 2020.

[PDF] [video]

We develop a conditional imitation learning approach, which can efficiently learn to drive using only 30 hours of human demonstrations. We evaluate our system in highly complex European urban roads with little traffic, which lack the structure and simplicity of roads used in prior work on IL for driving.

Information-Directed Exploration for Deep Reinforcement Learning.
Nikolay Nikolov, Johannes Kirschner, Felix Berkenkamp, Andreas Krause.
 International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR) 2019.

[PDF]

We propose to use Information-Directed Sampling (IDS) for exploration in reinforcement learning. Unlike classical exploration strategies such as UCB and Thompson sampling, our method can account both for parametric uncertainty and heteroscedasticity in the return distribution. The resulting strategy achieves a significant improvement over C51, QR-DQN and IQN on the Atari games domain.

2018

Efficient Octree-Based Volumetric SLAM Supporting Signed-Distance and Occupancy Mapping.
Emanuele Vespa, Nikolay Nikolov, Marius Grimm, Luigi Nardi, Paul H J Kelly, Stefan Leutenegger.
 IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA) 2018.
IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters, Vol: 3, Pages: 1144-1151.

[PDF] [video]

We propose a bayesian formulation for fusing depth camera measurements in a volumetric occupancy octree grid. Our approach is particulary targeting SLAM applications and allows direct surface extraction from the octree. We also provide a CPU-optimized octree library for 3D reconstruction and SLAM that aims to allow implementing general-purpose real-time mapping algorithms.
